Recess time and activities

Recess

Recess time is 20-25 mins. They have indoor recess and outdoor recess so you have fun all the time! They have a huge playground with slides, swings, monkeybars etc. Common recess activities Chase games: tag, chase, hide and go seek Ball games: four square, basketball, wall ball, soccer, kick ball Jumping/verbal games: jump rope, Chinese jump rope, hop scotch, hula hoops, chanting/clapping/rhyming games, Duck Duck Goose General play: make believe or fantasy play, or solitary play.

Awards Of JCE

We are excited to share that today we received news that Johns Creek Elementary has received two 2016 Single Statewide Accountability System (SSAS) awards. The hard work demonstrated by our students, faculty and community have earned our school the Platinum Award in the Greatest Gains category and a Platinum Award in the Highest Performing category. Both of these awards recognize JCE for exhibiting high growth and overall achievement for the past three years in the top 1 % of schools in the state. For additional information and criteria for each award please visit http://tinyurl.com/jcwmypo and for a list of all 2016 recipients visit http://tinyurl.com/hy6sqwp."

    The mission of the Lambert High School Wellness Program is to ensure that every student, staff and community member has the skills necessary to lead healthy, productive, fulfilling lives that not only improve their own well-being but positively impact the lives of those around them. This will be accomplished by the implementation of a comprehensive program that promotes wellness through educational and outreach activities. Whats New?

  • Covid 19 measures

    School will never be the same. All new safeguards will need to be installed on how to handle a health crisis or outbreak (from COVID-19 to something else) along with other disasters (natural or otherwise). What was previously inconceivable came fast this year, and has forever changed how we perceive educational centers, but also, I believe how schools will deliver learning to meet the diverse needs of their students.

  • Classroom changes

    Hygiene, physical and emotional, is essential education. In 2016, NPR reported the rise of anxiety and mental/emotional issues in students across the United States. With the advent of the pandemic, more than 50 percent of students surveyed reported needing mental health services since the school closures (ACLU of Southern California, 2020). Best Colleges surveyed households with a High School or College student, and more than 44 percent of students reported worried if they can enroll or stay in college (2020). From my perspective, the time has indisputably come to put emotional health and building resilience in every classroom, not just the well-funded ones.

  • Can COVID-19 outbreaks occur in schools?

    All students and employees must stay home if they have COVID-19 symptoms, tested positive for COVID-19 or had close contact with a person with COVID-19 within the last 14 days.
